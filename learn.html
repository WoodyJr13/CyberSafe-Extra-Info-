Key Cybersecurity Concepts

Phishing

Deceptive emails, texts, or cloned websites designed to steal your information. They often create a sense of urgency.

Social Engineering

The art of manipulating people into giving up confidential information. Tactics include pretexting, baiting, and tailgating.

White Hat Hacking

Ethical hackers who find security vulnerabilities before malicious attackers do. A growing and important career path.

Password Safety

Use long, unique passphrases for each account and store them securely in a password manager. Avoid common words.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

A second layer of security. Use authenticator apps or physical security keys over SMS for the best protection.

Scam/Fraud Calls

Deceptive phone calls attempting to trick you into revealing personal information or sending money, often by impersonating authorities or family members in distress.

Common Red Flags

Scammers use common tricks to fool you. Here’s what to look out for in suspicious messages and websites.

Urgent or Threatening Language

Phrases like "Account Locked," "Suspicious Activity," or "Action Required" are used to create panic and rush you into making a mistake.

Suspicious Links & Attachments

Always check the sender's email address and hover over links before clicking. Look for misspellings like "yourbankk.com" or weird URLs. Never open attachments you weren't expecting, especially from unknown senders, as they can contain malware like viruses or ransomware.

Generic Greetings

Legitimate companies will usually address you by your name. Vague greetings like "Dear Customer" or "Valued Member" are a red flag.
